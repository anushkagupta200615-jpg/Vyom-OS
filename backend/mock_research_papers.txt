Title: Early Detection of Urban Heat Islands Using Edge-Processed Satellite Imagery
Authors: Dr. A. Sharma, Dr. M. Gupta (ISRO-Simulated Research)
Abstract:
Urban Heat Islands (UHIs) present a severe risk to dense city centers. Real-time detection using edge-processed satellite imagery allows for immediate infrastructure mitigation. The Vyom OS framework demonstrates that thermal anomalies exceeding 40°C in urban grids require immediate deployment of cooling centers.
Key Findings:
1. When grid cell temperature gradient > 5°C relative to surrounding cells, structural heat absorption is critical.
2. Recommended mitigation: Deploy emergency water sprinklers and open cooling centers in affected grids.
3. Prediction model indicates a 30% spread to adjacent grids within 24 hours if unmitigated.

Title: Predictive Modelling of Forest Fires Using Multi-Spectral Sensor Data
Authors: R. Singh, K. Patel
Abstract:
Forest fires expand rapidly. Multi-spectral sensors on low-earth orbit satellites can identify pre-ignition thermal signatures.
Key Findings:
1. Rapid expansion is expected when dry vegetation index is high coupled with wind speeds > 15 km/h.
2. Data should be prioritized and compressed for earth transmission to reduce latency.
3. Mitigation Directive: Dispatch aerial firefighting units to perimeter grids and establish firebreaks immediately.

Title: Oceanic Oil Spill Detection and Trajectory Forecasting
Authors: L. Chen, M. Rodriguez
Abstract:
Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) combined with edge AI provides near-instantaneous detection of maritime oil spills. Oil slicks dampen surface capillary waves, presenting as dark anomalies on SAR imagery.
Key Findings:
1. Slicks expand according to local ocean currents and wind vectors.
2. Immediate response is required if slicks approach within 50km of coastal ecosystems.
3. Mitigation Directive: Deploy chemical dispersants and containment booms within a 10km radius of the epicenter.

Title: Unregistered Vessel Activity and Illegal Fishing Monitoring
Authors: H. Yamamoto
Abstract:
Satellites equipped with RF sensors and optical arrays can detect "dark fleets"—vessels operating with disabled AIS transponders.
Key Findings:
1. High correlation between dark vessel clusters and marine protected areas.
2. Edge ML can classify vessel types based on wake patterns.
3. Mitigation Directive: Dispatch coast guard interceptors to provided coordinates and alert regional maritime authorities.

Title: Methane Plume Detection from Industrial Facilities
Authors: S. Dubois, J. Smith
Abstract:
High-resolution hyperspectral imaging from orbit isolates localized methane emission plumes invisible to the naked eye.
Key Findings:
1. Methane super-emitters account for a disproportionate amount of greenhouse gas emissions.
2. Plumes detected exceeding 100 kg/hr require immediate on-ground verification.
3. Mitigation Directive: Issue automated regulatory alerts to facility operators and dispatch ground inspection teams to halt leaks.

Title: ISRO Space Weather Advisory Protocol for Aditya-L1 and ISRO Constellation
Authors: Space Environment Control Board (SECB), ISRO
Abstract:
Standard operating procedures for managing high-energy particle events, Coronal Mass Ejections (CMEs), and severe solar flares affecting ISRO spacecraft (RESOURCESAT, CARTOSAT, Aditya-L1).
Key Findings:
1. When an X-class solar flare is detected by DONKI/Aditya-L1 SUIT instrument, high-risk deep space anomalies are highly probable within 15 to 45 minutes.
2. South Atlantic Anomaly (SAA) operations: ISRO satellites crossing the SAA during a severe geomagnetic storm (Kp > 5) MUST power down sensitive optical payloads immediately to prevent Single Event Upsets (SEU).
3. Mitigation Directive: Enter Safe Mode. Shut down high-voltage systems. Orient solar panels edge-on to the incoming radiation flux. NavIC signals may degrade; switch to backup ground telemetry.

Title: Mitigation of D-Region Absorption and HF Radio Blackout in Ground Operations
Authors: ISRO Ground Command (ISTRAC, Bengaluru)
Abstract:
Analysis of communication degradation between ISTRAC ground stations and LEO satellites during severe X-ray flux events. Solar X-rays ionize the D-region of the ionosphere, absorbing High Frequency (HF) radio waves on the sunlit hemisphere.
Key Findings:
1. Sunlit hemisphere ground stations experience up to 90% signal loss during M5+ and X-class flares.
2. Blackout duration perfectly mirrors the X-ray flare curve (typically 20-60 minutes).
3. Mitigation Directive: Immediately route critical satellite telemetry through relay networks on the night-side hemisphere. Delay non-essential command uplink until flux levels drop below M1 class.

Title: Cascading Spacecraft Anomalies and Multi-Satellite Correlation
Authors: Spacecraft Health Monitoring Division, ISRO
Abstract:
Analysis of correlated anomaly events across the ISRO fleet (Chandrayaan-3, INSAT-3DR, NVS-01). Identifying cascading failures triggered by systemic space weather events rather than isolated hardware degradation.
Key Findings:
1. If two or more spacecraft report attitude control errors, star tracker blinding, or unexpected reboots within a 15-minute window, it is classified as a Cascading Space Weather Event.
2. These events are almost exclusively tied to sudden high-density solar wind shockwaves arriving at Earth.
3. Mitigation Directive: Halt all scheduled orbital maneuvers. Engage autonomous star-tracker recovery loops and cross-verify attitude using gyroscopes only.
